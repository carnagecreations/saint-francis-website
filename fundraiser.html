The future: a real facility

Every “we’re full” is an animal we couldn’t save.

The call comes in — a dog dumped in the desert, a horse headed to slaughter, a litter nobody wants. Most days we have to say no. Not because we don’t care, but because we’ve run out of room. A proper kennel facility here in Yuma County changes that answer: real housing, secure fencing, quarantine space for the sick ones, and the capacity to say yes when it matters most.

This is the line between a rescue that scrapes by and one that outlives its founders. It won’t happen on $25 gifts alone — it takes major donors, grants, and people who want their name on something permanent. If you can give at that level, or know someone who can, this is the conversation that builds it.
